What is Pi?

Pi is the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter.

Pi is commonly reffered to as 3.14

Facts about Pi

The Pi Symbol

The symbol for Pi has been used for over 250 years.

The Exact Value of Pi

Since the exact value of Pi can never be calculated, we can never find the accurate area or circumference of a circle.

March 14

March 14 or 3/14 is celebrated as Pi Day because 3.14 are the first digits of Pi.

The World Record

The record for reciting the most number of decimal places of Pi was achieved by Rajveer Meena at VIT University. He was able to recite 70,000 decimal places.

A Summary of Pi

Pi is commonly referred to as 3.14

And is the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter. It is widely believed that pi was first calculated by Archimedes of Syracuse in the ancient world. Pi is also used in many different formulas such as the circumfrence of a circle (2 x pi x radius). Pi can be used for simple tasks like finding the area, and can also be used for more complex acts like calculating circular velocities.
